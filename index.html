What We Do

Our Services

From field sampling to laboratory analysis and expert reporting, we offer end-to-end microanalytical support.

Phytolith Analysis

Identification and interpretation of silicified plant microfossils from archaeological and geological sediments to reconstruct past vegetation, land use, and agricultural practices.

Microanalytical Reporting

Comprehensive laboratory reporting for development-led archaeology, academic research, and environmental impact assessments, prepared to professional publication standards.

Starch Grain Analysis

Recovery and identification of ancient starch granules from artefact residues and sediments, providing direct evidence of food processing and plant exploitation.

Pollen & Micro-debris Studies

Palynological and micro-debris analysis to build multi-proxy environmental and cultural records, supporting site interpretation and landscape reconstruction.

Field Sampling & Strategy

Expert guidance on sampling strategies, field collection protocols, and project design to ensure optimal recovery of micro-botanical and microanalytical evidence.

Academic & Research Collaboration

Partnership on research projects, co-authored publications, conference presentations, and postgraduate supervision in micro-botanical disciplines.

Our Work

Selected Projects

A selection of recent and notable projects demonstrating our range of expertise.

Phytolith Analysis

Neolithic Farming Landscape, Southern England

Multi-phase phytolith analysis across a large-scale development site, identifying early cereal cultivation and trackway construction through micro-botanical evidence preserved in buried soils.

Starch Analysis

Bronze Age Grinding Stone Residues, Mediterranean

Starch grain extraction and identification from grinding stone surfaces, revealing dietary staples and food processing techniques across a multi-period settlement complex.

Multi-proxy

Wetland Environmental Reconstruction, Northern Europe

Integrated phytolith, pollen, and micro-debris analysis of waterlogged sediment sequences, contributing to a comprehensive palaeoenvironmental reconstruction of a regionally significant wetland site.

Research Collaboration

Ancient Crop Dispersal Study, East Africa

Collaborative research investigating the spread of domesticated crops through phytolith morphometrics, contributing to peer-reviewed publications on early agricultural exchange networks.

Who We Are

About Us

Micro Botanical Insights is a specialist consulting practice providing expert microanalytical services to the archaeological, heritage, and environmental sectors, based at the University of Wollongong.

Founded by Kelsey and Haidee, the practice brings together years of academic research and commercial experience, delivering rigorous scientific methodology to every project — from large-scale developer-funded excavations to focused research collaborations.

Our team holds advanced qualifications in archaeobotany, palaeoecology, and microscopy, with particular expertise in phytolith analysis, starch grain identification, and multi-proxy environmental reconstruction.

We work with archaeological units, universities, heritage consultancies, and government agencies across Australia and internationally.
